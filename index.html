Hello There!

This website presents information on some of the topics that I find interesting.

I'm convinced that the topics covered on this website, if solved or developed further, has the potential to make the world a better place. Specifically in terms of quality of life (perhaps HDI is a reasonable proxy), and allowing humans to progress to a more technologically advanced and humane civilisation. There are many such topics, but I've narrowed down to a few that I am most passionate about at this stage.


Intent

My hope is that the content here serves the audience in such a way that it inspires them to work on them and contribute to solving/developing them.

While there are many excellent resources (some of which I reference in the content) on these topics, I wanted to present the topics freely, in a manner I prefer, and interlace some of my personal thoughts or annotations where appropriate.

The final intent of this effort is so that I can better understand the topics myself, by presenting it to others (Learning by teaching).


Website layout and implementation

The navigation links at the top take you to the home-page for the topics. The content is arranged in a hierarchical manner (tree), generally, so the navigation links below should help guide with intended flow of content consumption. However, this is a subjective preference, and you can use the table of contents to serve you the way you desire. There may be multiple tables of contents, where the hierarchy is arranged differently (categorised perhaps by areas of physics, reliability of information, etc.).

You can usually distinguish between conventionally accepted information and the author's annotations/opinion/speculation by checking the references or links. Some of the content is explicitly marked with different font styles to help distinguish between the two.

The vast majority of the website content is just html and css, with the intent to widen reach and minimise distractions from the content. Calculators and scripts may be present on certain pages, for demonstrational or educational purposes.
