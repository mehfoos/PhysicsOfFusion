Hello There!

This website presents information on some of the topics that I find interesting.

I'm convinced that the topics covered on this website, if solved or developed further, has the potential to make the world a better place. Specifically in terms of quality of life (perhaps HDI is a reasonable proxy), and allowing humans to progress to a more technologically advanced and humane civilisation. There are many such topics, but I've narrowed down to a few that I am most passionate about at this stage.

For scientific or engineering challenges, I try to approach the topic from first principles, and cover as many branches as I could for the “what-if”/”how about” scenarios, to ensure that every possible avenue for solving a problem is explored.


Intent

My hope is that the content here serves the audience in such a way that it inspires them to contribute to the field. Again, for scientific or engineering challenges, I suspect that breakthroughs may come through old ideas that were discarded due to limitations in the past whose re-evaluation today may make it attractive given the technological and scientific progress in other areas.

While there are many excellent resources (some of which I reference in the content) on these topics, I wanted to present the topics freely, in a manner I prefer, and interlace some of my personal thoughts or annotations where appropriate.

Another intent of this effort is to help me better understand the topics myself, by presenting it to others (Learning by teaching).


Website layout and implementation

Navigation Links

The navigation links at the top take you to the home-page for the topics. The content is arranged in a hierarchical manner (tree), generally, so the navigation links below should help guide with intended flow of content consumption. However, this is a subjective preference, and you can use the table of contents to serve you the way you desire. There may be multiple tables of contents, where the hierarchy is arranged differently (categorised perhaps by areas of physics, reliability of information, etc.).

The vast majority of the website content is just html and css, with the intent to widen reach and minimise distractions from the content. Calculators and scripts may be present on certain pages, for demonstration or educational purposes.

Font Style:

You can usually distinguish between conventionally accepted information and the author's annotations/opinion/speculation by checking the references or links. Some of the content is explicitly marked with different font styles to help distinguish between the two.
  • Derived from a collection of authoritative sources and conveying conventionally accepted information.
  • The author’s subjective opinions, newly published research or content with low confidence on accuracy.

Collapsible Sections:

There may be collapsible sections that provide more details on a topic. The intent here is to provide the reader with the choice of abstraction of detail. Detailed information, including any mathematical equations and links to related content are generally included in collapsible sections.
